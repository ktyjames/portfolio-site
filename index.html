acbj.com

Print to digital - Building the future of a Business News Organization

Redesigning the outdated ACBJ.com site was pretty high up on my 'omgcanIplzdothis' list. After learning as much about the company as possible in 6 months it was official. I was going to be the lead UX designer on the project.

I started in Google Analytics, and wow, the numbers I saw were pretty terrible. The bounce rate of the homepage was 80% and the average PV time was 53 seconds. After some digging I found that users spent most of their time on the Careers page. For me, this solidified the need for a more optimized way of posting and applying for jobs- something that the old site was severely lacking.

ACBJ.com's initial wireframes

With my audience and their needs determined I could define some business goals. My new design needed to inform users who ACBJ was, as well as why they would want to work there. I presented stakeholders with a creative brief and wireframes. My plan was to slim down the content, focusing only on the essentials.

The prototype, built by me with Bootstrap and Sass

When it came to the Careers page I worked with devs to set up a feed that connected with our intranet. This meant that every time a new job was posted internally, it would automatically appear on the Careers page. This lead to a huge increase in people who were applying for job listings through the site.

Ultimately, the design was art directed by stakeholders more than expected. The visuals are something I'm not 100% happy with, but in the end I was glad to have modernized it. The final result was cleaner- and it was organized for users who wanted to learn more about the company. It was also no longer housed within a clunky CMS, so it ran faster and was easier to implement.

Crema

Connecting coffee lovers with local roasters and cafés

Crema is for caffeine lovers who are passionate about the quality of their coffee. I worked with two close friends over the course a year to build this product from the ground up. I was the lead designer while they handled all the engineering and development work.

Crema's primary goal aims to provide users with an easy way to discover and connect with local cafés. After logging in, the landing screen displays a list of local coffee shops nearby. Selecting one will take you to the shop page, where you can view a variety of information. From here, users then have the ability to favorite, rate, or write a story about a shop. Favoriting allows them to receive push notifications that get delivered to their in-app inbox. Next is Me, which shows what the user favorited as well as any ratings or stories written.

As work on the app began, the goals for Crema evolved into something twofold. After building the initial experience we found it also had the potential to benefit shop owners. When you own a small business, marketing doesn't come cheap. But what if you could reach out to your audience directly and still be within budget? We saw the perfect opportunity to monotize this- leading to a web application that focused on shop owners and their needs.

Landing Page

The Overview page shows registered owners vital statistics gathered from the Crema iOS component. These stats consist of how many users visited their page or favorited their shop; how many messages the shop sent, and in relation, how many of those messages were opened. Other modules on this page include how many stories have been written to date, as well as a live feed of the most recent user stories.

Message Center
My Shop

Spam was a problem we tried to solve for when it came to the idea of owners sending messages to users. In an attempt to reduce the chance of spam, push notifications require a subscription. We set up a pricing model made up of a free trial and three different subscription tiers. This way owners wouldn't be as likely to abuse the messaging system.

The Shop page is for owners to edit any info about their shop that displays in the iOS app. Things such as photo, shop name, hours, and menu are all updated in real time once edited.

Hemmings Daily

The world's leading classic car news source

Not long after being hired at ACBJ, I was asked if I'd be interested in redesigning Hemmings' blog. The project consisted of designing the blog's landing page and article pages. I had never done anything quite this big before but was happy to take on the challenge.

Our initial meeting consisted of listing out requirements and goals. The homepage for Hemmings was redesigned not long before, so we needed to keep the look and feel consistent. Their main concerns were to increase social sharing, increase comments, and surface more content. The main stakeholder I worked with on this was open to trying out a lot of cool ideas, and much of my early concepts reflected that.

Hemmings Daily inital concepts

After trying a bunch of different ideas I came up with a concept that hit all the goals they were trying to achieve. I kept the Blog landing page similar to Hemmings' current homepage in order to keep the brand consistent. The only big change to note on that page is the redesign of their ad and promotional modules. These make the whole experience flow much smoother and not cause as many interruptions.

Landing Page
Article Page

The Article page itself was a complete overhaul. The images span the full with of the page, and ads are quietly nested out of the way. I took several design aspects from their iOS app to further establish a sense of their brand. Underneath the article there is a 'Related Blog Posts' module- intended to keep users engaged with more content without having to search for it. Moving a bit farther down the page I also tackled their comments section. The original comments section was messy and underdesigned. Hemmings Daily has a lot of active users, so it was important to make the experience as easy as possible.

Mixlab

Bring the party with you - a pocket DJ app

Mixlab is a fun and simple app I designed with the same friends I worked with on Crema. Meant to be a party app, you can choose songs from your music library and mix them with Mixlab's sounds. All you have to do is upload whichever song you want to play and start experimenting. The app comes pre-loaded with 3 sound packs and there are many others available to buy- making the possibilities endless.
